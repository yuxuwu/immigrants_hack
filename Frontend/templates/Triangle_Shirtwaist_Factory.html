Triangle Shirtwaist Factory

It was March 25th, 1911 when the Triangle Shirtwaist Factory that you're standing by was engulfed by a massive inferno. The workers within the factory, mostly comprised of 123 women that were Italian and Jewish immigrants, faced the unthinkable choice of falling to their deaths or being burned alive. As a consequence of this industrial disaster, many reforms mandating better building access, exits, alarm systems, and a plethora of new reforms improving work conditions were introduced into the state of New York.
